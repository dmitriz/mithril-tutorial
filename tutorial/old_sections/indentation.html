A Note About Hyperscript Indentation

In all templating languages, figuring out a good indentation strategy is no joke. You (and your teammates) need to be able to read and comprehend your code as quickly as possible. Mithril's hyperscript is no exception.

Certainly, you'll find your own, preferred style. That said, I find that for any but the simplest structures, a multi-line, comma-first approach leads to the most readable code.

I follow a very simple set of guidelines:

  1. New line for every new m(), preceded by its comma
  2. New line for every attributes object, preceded by its comma
  3. Normal formatting for functions within attributes objects
  4. All of these rules can be ignored for very simple structures, where new lines would just make it harder to read, e.g. m('', {title: 'text title'}, 'Some text')

In this way, the resulting HTML's nesting is an almost perfect mirror of our hyperscript; the only meaningful exceptions being hyperscript's attribute objects. 

var rootVnode = m("main"
	, m("header"
		, m("h1", "This is my header")
	)
	, m("section"
		, { class: "body-content" }
		, m("p", "This is my body content")
	)
	, m("footer", "This is my footer")
)

m.render(document.body, rootVnode)
